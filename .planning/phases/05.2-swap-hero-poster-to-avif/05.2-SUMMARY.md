## Why the gate stays red

The dominant fix (AVIF) closed 79% of the gap on commit 1. Commits 2 + 3 chased diagnostic leads from subsequent LHCI re-runs that turned out to be wrong; the changes are still defensible long-term (one removes a redundant fetch, the other prevents a hypothetical font-swap LCP candidate) but did not move the simulator's projection.

The remaining 1,569 ms is not asset-level. The H1 LCP element paints at 1,254 ms unthrottled (real-network reality). The 3,869 ms number is LHCI's "simulated" projection of how the page's resource graph + CPU graph would perform on Case C throttling. The remaining levers (lazy-load GSAP + ScrollTrigger, conditional General Sans preload, switch LHCI throttling method, or relax the gate to the 2,500 ms CLAUDE.md §12 spec floor) are structural and explicitly out of scope for this phase.

Full diagnosis with public LHR URL, phase comparison, and recommended next moves: `docs/m5-phase-5-lhci-run.md`.
