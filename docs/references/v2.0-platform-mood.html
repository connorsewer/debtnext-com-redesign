Start here.

An orientation in two screens. Read this even if you intend to skip the rest.

DebtNext builds dPlat, the recovery operating system for enterprise credit originators. We orchestrate $100M+ in monthly placements across 70+ banks, utilities, and debt owners.

Founded 2003, ran independently for 22 years. In September 2025, Transworld Systems Inc. (TSI) acquired the company. We remain a dedicated SaaS business unit with an audited information wall between the platform and TSI's collection services.

This brand lives in a category full of sameness. Traditional vendors look like 2015 enterprise SaaS: safe blue gradients, stock photography of headsets, templated layouts. DebtNext does not. We look like a modern platform company, because that is what we are.

Six things that matter most

  • Dark canvas is the default. Every flagship surface (home, platform, features, case studies) opens in dark. Light mode exists for documentation, email, and long-form reading, but it is not the dominant identity.
  • Indigo is the signal. oklch(0.55 0.21 268). One saturated color, used with discipline. It carries primary CTAs, active states, key data points, focus rings, and the chart's primary series. Never two signals in one composition.
  • Show product, do not decorate. Dashboard mockups, workflow nodes, vendor scorecards, and live data tiles support the adjacent copy. Avoid generative visuals that do not clarify the story.
  • Pill geometry for actions, sharp geometry for content. Primary buttons are pill-shaped. Cards on dark are flat with no radius. Product mockups keep an 8px chrome.
  • Motion is calm and load-bearing. Fade-ups, accordion transitions, hover micro-interactions. No bounce, no parallax, no scaling. Every animation respects prefers-reduced-motion.
  • One primary CTA per band. Each hero, section, card group, or nav row contains exactly one filled signal-color action. Secondary actions are ghost, text, or muted.

01

Why DebtNext exists.

The problem we solve, in plain English. The story behind every page that follows.

A major financial institution or utility has millions of delinquent accounts spread across 10 to 30+ vendors. Collection agencies, law firms, skip tracing services, debt buyers. Each vendor has its own reporting cadence, data formats, compliance posture. The institution's internal team manages this with spreadsheets, email, SharePoint, and a homegrown database from a decade ago that nobody fully understands anymore.

The result is predictable. Poor visibility into portfolio performance. Inconsistent vendor accountability. Compliance gaps that surface in exams. Money left on the table because nobody can see the whole picture. That is the problem we built DebtNext to solve.

The platform doesn't care who owns it. It cares about the vendor scorecard.

Rob Novosel, Co-founder

Why indigo

Indigo at oklch(0.55 0.21 268) reads as the highest-trust action color in the modern fintech vocabulary. It carries enough chroma to function as a single signal at small scale, holds against both the deep dark canvas and a cream light surface without shifting hue, and avoids both the violet AI-tool reflex and the navy enterprise reflex. One indigo. One job: action, focus, the most important data point.

Theme behavior

Toggle via the sun/moon control top-right. Tokens re-bind, nothing recompiles. The dark canvas (the default) carries the brand's premium fintech identity. Light mode exists for documentation, email, long-form reading, and print contexts but should not become the dominant identity on flagship surfaces.

Color usage rules

  • Signal under 10% of any surface. One filled primary CTA per band. No competing indigo elements.
  • Tinted neutrals only. No #000, no #fff. Canvas tints toward violet at h≈280.
  • No gradient text. Solid colors. Hierarchy by weight and size.
  • Hierarchy through tonal contrast. Canvas → Elevated → Strong. Layer dark surfaces; do not lean on shadows.
  • Body text contrast. All pairings pass WCAG 2.2 AA. --fg on --canvas = 14.2:1.
07

Typography.

One sans for body and display. One mono for figures and code.

Typography is intentionally restrained. General Sans handles every proportional setting from caption to display. JetBrains Mono handles every figure, code block, and metadata label. Hierarchy comes from scale and weight, not from family-mixing.

The two families

FamilyRoleWhere it appears
General SansUI & displayHeadlines, body, buttons, navigation, callouts, table cells, hero deck. The default for everything except figures.
JetBrains MonoFigures & metadataKPI numbers ($100M+, 22, 70+), code, eyebrows, table headers, section numbers, the nav search shortcut hint.

Why General Sans

General Sans is a contemporary humanist sans with strong character at display weights and clean rendering at body size. It carries enough personality at hero scale to feel intentional, holds at 14px UI sizes without thinning, and avoids both the Inter-as-default reflex and the geometric-sans uniformity of the SaaS category. It also reads well across both the dark canvas and the cream light surface.

Typography rules

  • Body line length 60-70ch. Caps at --max-prose: 68ch.
  • Weights 400, 480, 500, 600. Sparingly. 400 for body, 480 for display emphasis, 500 for UI emphasis, 600 for wordmark.
  • Letter spacing normal or slightly tight. -0.02em at display, 0 at body. No aggressive negative tracking.
  • No all-caps body. Eyebrows and short labels only.
  • Mono for numbers in figures and tables. Tabular figures via font-feature-settings: 'tnum'.

09

Imagery.

Dashboard mockups first. Atmospheric secondary. No stock photography.

Category 1 · Dashboard mockups (preferred)

The strongest imagery on every flagship surface is a recognizable slice of dPlat: a vendor scorecard, a KPI tile grid, a placement decision panel, a compliance event timeline. See the live mockups in section 14 for the canonical patterns. Use real-feeling but anonymized data. Optimize for responsive cropping.

Category 2 · Atmospheric and generative

Dark cinematic backgrounds, indigo radial gradients on hero blocks, abstract orchestration-lattice motifs in SVG. Atmospheric imagery sets the premium fintech tone without competing with product visuals.

Category 3 · Custom illustration

Commissioned. Geometric, line-based, monochrome with a single indigo accent. Used for editorial features, integration diagrams, complex concept explainers. Approved illustrators only.

Category 4 · Photography (emergency use only)

Last resort. If used: cool-toned, journalistic distance, no handshakes, no headsets, no business attire portraits. Approval required from Gian.

Imagery treatment rules

Critical hero media is preloaded only when it materially improves LCP. Below-fold imagery lazy-loads. Decorative atmospheric images use empty alt text. Meaningful product screenshots get descriptive alt. Charts get text summaries near the visual or in aria-describedby.

11

Motion.

Calm, fluid, load-bearing. Animation is a brand element. Click any demo below to play.

Motion is a core brand element, not an afterthought. It signals platform sophistication, communicates state change, and earns reader attention without demanding it. We use animation deliberately: hero reveals, accordion transitions, hover micro-interactions, counter animations on KPIs, fade-ups on scroll, and ambient breathing on the orchestration lattice.

Motion philosophy

  • Calm, not loud. Easing is cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.7, 0.2, 1), a long deceleration curve. No bounce, no elastic, no overshoot.
  • Confirmatory, not decorative. Every animation confirms a state change the user initiated, or reveals content as it enters the viewport.
  • Fast at the small scale, slower at the narrative scale. Hover and focus: 150ms. Accordion and panel: 300ms. Hero reveals: 500ms.
  • Property restraint. Animate opacity, transform, background-color, color, and border-color. Avoid width, height, top, left.
  • Reduced motion is a first-class citizen. prefers-reduced-motion: reduce disables every entrance animation and keeps state changes immediate.

Accordion (live)

The canonical pattern for revealing one capability at a time without changing the visual field.

Places accounts with the right vendor automatically, based on segment, performance history, and compliance standing. Rule changes deploy without IT.

Replaces manual reconciliation, monthly QBR-by-PowerPoint, and "I cannot answer that question without a week of data gathering." Every vendor scored on identical data, refreshed automatically.

Captures every placement, every decision, every complaint, every payment. When a regulator asks for oversight evidence, the answer is a query.

Reveal-on-scroll

Sections fade up as they enter the viewport, 24px translate over 600ms with ease-standard. The effect is calm and load-bearing: a reader who scrolls past quickly sees no animation lag; a reader who slows to read sees the page assemble itself around them. IntersectionObserver-driven; respects prefers-reduced-motion.

Counter animation on KPIs

The figures in section 01 ($100M+, 22, 70+) count up from 0 to target on first viewport entry. Played once per session via IntersectionObserver. The number is animated, not the layout, so there is no shift. Scroll back to section 01 in a fresh tab to see it play.

What we do not animate

  • Layout properties. No width, height, top, left. Always transform.
  • Bounce or elastic. Decel curves only.
  • Parallax. Doesn't earn its cost.
  • Hero images on scroll. Never.
  • Auto-rotating carousels without pause control. Inaccessible.
  • Scale-on-hover for cards. Border or color shift is enough.
12

Writing & tone.

The voice system. The strongest discipline in the brand.

Confident, specific, never marketing-speak. We sound like a senior operator who has run this play before, talking to another senior operator.

Core principles

  • Mechanism, then proof, then provocation. Name what the system does. Show the number. Only then say something bold.
  • One specific noun per sentence. "Spreadsheets" beats "tools." "Seven agencies" beats "multiple vendors."
  • The fear stack does the opening. Lead with the current-state pain, not the platform's feature.
  • Numbers in mono. Tabular figures. Always.
  • Active voice. "dPlat orchestrates placements" not "Placements are orchestrated by dPlat."

Hard rules (non-negotiable)

  • No em dashes. Use commas, colons, semicolons, periods, or parentheses.
  • No "not X, it's Y." Pick the right thing the first time.
  • No three-adjective phrases. One adjective or none.
  • No exclamation points. Ever.
  • No emoji. Anywhere in product or marketing surfaces.
  • Sentence case. Headlines and UI labels. No all-caps body.

The banned phrase list

never write

AI-powered. Compliance-first. Omnichannel. Consumer-centric. Data-driven. Digital-first. Comprehensive. Robust. Seamless. Next-generation. Industry-leading. World-class. Cutting-edge. Best-in-class. Mission-critical. Empower. Unlock. Elevate. Transform. Optimize. Accelerate. Streamline.

Required vocabulary

  • System of record. The frame for what dPlat is.
  • Orchestrate. What dPlat does to vendors and placements.
  • Recovery operating system. The tagline. Don't dilute.
  • Vendor scorecard. The artifact dPlat replaces.
  • Information wall. The audited separation between dPlat and TSI.
  • Decision Engine. The named capability. Capitalize.
  • Post-charge-off. The market segment.

Dashboard usage rules

  • Real-feeling, anonymized data. No real client names, account numbers, or PII.
  • Indigo for the primary chart series. Other series use the chart palette (success, warn, info).
  • Live indicators where the system breathes. The pulsing dot, the "Updated 2 min ago" timestamp, the active activity feed.
  • Animate bars and lines on scroll into view. Once per page-load. Played via IntersectionObserver.
  • Print-safe. Dashboard mockups should remain legible when printed in grayscale.

Website (debtnext.com) patterns

  • Header navigation. Dark, sticky, 72px tall, 5 top-level links max, exactly one filled primary CTA. See the nav at the top of this page.
  • Hero. Concise headline (under 18 chars per line), short deck (1-2 sentences), attached email/demo form or single pill CTA, atmospheric or product media. See the hero on this page.
  • Feature accordion. Reveal one capability at a time. Active item updates a paired product visual. See section 11 for the live pattern.
  • Dashboard band. Real product visual, full-bleed or framed. The vendor scorecard mockup above is the canonical example.
  • Social proof. Quiet section. Testimonial card on dark with --canvas-elev background. No carousel without pause control.
  • Benefit deep-dives. 2-4 sections, each one concept. Alternate dark / elevated dark / dark. One CTA per band.
  • Final CTA. Strong dark band. One filled primary action. Short conversion copy.
  • Footer. Comprehensive grouped links, muted dark surface, TSI disclosure, copyright.

LinkedIn

  • Hero card on dark canvas. Wordmark with indigo node, no decorative imagery.
  • Posts open with a specific number or named persona, never a generic claim.
  • Carousels: 5–7 slides at 1080×1350. First slide is the hook (one sentence + one number). Last slide is the CTA.
  • No emoji, no exclamation points, no "thrilled to announce."

Email & transactional

  • Light theme (cream paper). Rendering across email clients is too inconsistent for dark.
  • General Sans with Helvetica Neue / Arial fallback.
  • Signature: name, role, wordmark, "A TSI Company" line. No banner, no calendar widget.
  • Transactional emails follow the same shell as marketing email; copy stays product-specific.

Video

  • Intro: 1.5 seconds. The luminous node fades in, scales to wordmark, holds.
  • Lower thirds: General Sans Medium 28px on dark.
  • Outro: tagline plus URL on dark. Two seconds. No music swell.

  2. Tier 2. New visual, layout, social card using existing system. Gian approves within 48 hours.
  3. Tier 3. Net-new headline, claim, statistic, tagline variation. Gian + Connor.
  4. Tier 4. Anything touching TSI, compliance, the information wall. Add Andrew.

Exception process

Exceptions exist. To request one, post in #brand-exceptions with the surface, the rule being broken, the reason, the proposed alternative, the expiration date. Gian approves or rejects within 48 hours. Approved exceptions are logged in the asset library and reviewed quarterly.

FAQ

Can I use a different font in a specific deck?

No. General Sans / JetBrains Mono only. If a partner deck constrains the fonts, request an exception via section 17.

Can I use indigo on every CTA?

Yes for primary actions and active states. No for secondary buttons, which stay neutral. One filled primary indigo per band, period.

Can I write "AI-powered" if it's accurate?

No. Name the specific capability: "Decision Engine," "Scorecard Analytics," "Workflow Rules."

Can I use an em dash if it's grammatically correct?

No. Use a comma, colon, semicolon, period, or parentheses.

Can I animate things outside the motion tokens?

No. The five tokens cover every animation in the system. Custom durations or curves require approval from Gian.
